### Core educational section

- Heading: The Affordable Care Act & Marketplace Insurance for Individuals & Families
- Supporting idea: Health insurance can be complex, and the page explains the ACA, the Health Insurance Marketplace, coverage options, and out-of-pocket costs.

### ACA points

- The ACA was signed into law in 2010.
- It was intended to make health insurance more affordable and accessible.
- It prohibits denials or higher pricing due to pre-existing conditions.
- It requires essential health benefits such as preventive care, mental health services, emergency services, prescription drugs, and maternity/newborn care.
- The page also says the individual mandate penalty was effectively eliminated in 2019.

### Marketplace points

- The Health Insurance Marketplace helps individuals, families, and small businesses shop for and enroll in coverage.
- The page points users to `healthcare.gov`.
- The live page says open enrollment "typically runs from November 1 through December 15 each year."

### Coverage tiers

- Bronze: lowest premiums, highest out-of-pocket costs.
- Silver: moderate premiums and costs; includes cost-sharing reductions for eligible shoppers.
- Gold: higher premiums, lower costs when using care.
- Platinum: highest premiums, lowest costs when using care.

### Out-of-pocket cost explainer

- Deductible
- Copayments and coinsurance
- Out-of-pocket maximum

### Subsidy section

- Heading: Understanding Subsidies: The Premium Tax Credit
- Explains the Premium Tax Credit for eligible low- and moderate-income individuals and families buying through the Marketplace.
- Eligibility points mentioned:
  - Household income at least 100% and not more than 400% of the federal poverty line
  - No access to affordable employer coverage
  - Not eligible for Medicaid, Medicare, or CHIP
  - Cannot be claimed as a dependent; married applicants generally file jointly
- Explains that the credit can reduce monthly premiums in advance and is reconciled at tax time.
- Advises reporting income or household-size changes to the Marketplace.

### Types of life insurance called out

- Term Life: Affordable coverage for set years
- Whole Life: Lifetime protection and cash value
- Final Expense: Peace of mind for loved ones
- Index Universal Life: Cash growth tied to indexes
- Long Term Care: Support for extended needs
- Short Term Care: Temporary healthcare support
- Group Life: Protecting employees together
- Critical Illness: Lump-sum for major illness

### What is life insurance

- Life insurance is described as a contract where premiums are paid in exchange for a lump-sum payment to beneficiaries upon death.
- The page frames it as a crucial part of financial planning and family stability.

### Why users need it

- The page describes life insurance as "love turned into money."
- It emphasizes protecting people who depend on the insured financially.

### Product detail snippets

- Term Life Insurance
  - Coverage for a specific period such as 10, 20, or 30 years
  - Affordable premiums
  - Option to convert to permanent coverage
- Whole Life Insurance
  - Lifetime coverage
  - Fixed premiums
  - Cash value accumulation
- Universal Life Insurance
  - Flexible premiums and death benefits
  - Potential for cash value growth
- Final Expense Insurance
  - Designed to cover funeral and burial costs
  - Simplified underwriting process
- Indexed Universal Life Insurance
  - Cash value tied to market indexes
  - Potential for higher returns with downside protection
- Survivorship Life Insurance
  - Covers two individuals, typically spouses
  - Pays after the death of the second insured

### Long-Term Disability page content

Source page: https://onepointinsuranceagency.com/long-term-disability-income

- Hero: Protect Your Paycheck with Long-Term Disability Income Insurance
- Main message: If illness or injury prevents you from working, this coverage helps replace income to support your family and lifestyle.
- Definition: Long-term disability income insurance is paycheck protection that usually replaces about 50-70% of regular salary when someone cannot work for months or years.
- Conditions mentioned:
  - Cancer
  - Stroke
  - Mental illness
  - Injuries
  - Chronic illness
- Reasons it matters:
  - Pay bills and mortgage
  - Protect your family
  - Cover illness and injury, not just accidents
  - Peace of mind during long recovery periods
- How it works:
  - Illness or injury happens
  - Elimination period of about 90-180 days
  - Monthly benefits begin
  - Benefits continue until recovery or end of coverage period
- Features called out:
  - Reliable income protection
  - Extended duration
  - Own-occupation protection
  - Flexible underwriting and riders
