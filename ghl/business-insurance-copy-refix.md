What Is General Liability Insurance?
General Liability Insurance is a foundational business insurance policy that helps protect businesses from third-party claims involving bodily injury, property damage, and certain personal or advertising injuries.

It is often one of the first policies a business purchases because many landlords, clients, vendors, and contracts require it.

In simple terms:
If your business is blamed for causing injury or damage to someone else, General Liability Insurance may help cover the cost of the claim, including legal defense and settlements, up to the policy limits.

What General Liability Insurance Typically Covers
1. Bodily Injury Liability
If someone is injured and your business is found responsible, this coverage may help with medical bills, legal costs, and settlements.

Example:
A customer walks into your office, slips on a wet floor, and suffers a back injury. They file a claim against your business for medical expenses and lost income.
Your General Liability policy may help respond to that claim.

2. Property Damage Liability
If your business causes damage to someone else’s property, this coverage may help pay for repairs or replacement.

Example:
You are a contractor working at a client’s location and accidentally damage expensive flooring, walls, or equipment during a job.
General Liability may help cover the damage if the claim is covered.

3. Personal and Advertising Injury
This can include certain claims like libel, slander, copyright infringement in advertising, or reputational harm tied to your advertising activities.

Example:
A competitor claims your marketing content damaged their reputation or used protected material improperly.
General Liability may help with covered legal defense costs.

4. Medical Payments
This coverage may help pay for minor medical expenses if someone is injured on your business premises, regardless of fault, subject to policy terms.

Example:
A visitor trips in your office lobby and needs urgent care for a minor injury.
Medical payments coverage may help with immediate medical costs.

5. Legal Defense Costs
Even if a claim is exaggerated or groundless, defending your business can be expensive. Legal fees alone can create major pressure on a small business.

General Liability Insurance can help with the cost of defending covered claims.

Frequently Asked Questions About General Liability Insurance
Is General Liability Insurance required by law?
Not always, but many landlords, clients, vendors, and contracts require it before allowing you to do business.

Is General Liability Insurance enough by itself?
Not always. Many businesses need additional protection such as workers compensation, commercial auto, property, professional liability, or cyber coverage.

Can startups get General Liability Insurance?
Yes. Startups often need coverage early, especially if they sign leases, work with clients, or want to look credible and protected from day one.

Can I get a certificate of insurance?
Yes, businesses often need certificates of insurance for contracts, landlords, or clients.

Does General Liability cover my business tools or building?
Usually no. That usually falls under commercial property or other specialized coverages.

💼 WHAT IS COMMERCIAL PROPERTY INSURANCE?
Commercial Property Insurance helps protect the physical assets of your business — including your building, equipment, inventory, furniture, and more — from covered risks.

Whether you own your building or lease your space, your business likely depends on physical assets to operate.

If those assets are damaged or destroyed, this coverage may help you recover.
🛡️ WHAT COMMERCIAL PROPERTY INSURANCE CAN COVER
1. Building Coverage
If you own your building, this helps protect the structure itself.

Example:
A fire damages your office building, including walls, roofing, and interior structure.
Commercial Property Insurance may help cover repair or rebuilding costs.

2. Business Personal Property (BPP)
Covers items inside your business.

✔ Equipment
✔ Inventory
✔ Furniture

✔ Tools
✔ Machinery

Example:

3. Equipment & Machinery

4. Inventory Protection
Especially important for retail and product-based businesses.

Example:

🧾 REPLACEMENT COST vs ACTUAL CASH VALUE (CRITICAL)
Not All Property Coverage Pays the Same
One of the biggest mistakes business owners make is not understanding how their property is valued during a claim.

Replacement Cost Coverage
Pays to replace damaged property at today’s cost.

✔ No deduction for depreciation
✔ Better protection
✔ Higher payout in a loss

Actual Cash Value (ACV)
Pays based on depreciated value.

❌ Lower payout
❌ Out-of-pocket gap
❌ Can leave you underfunded after a loss

Example:
You bought equipment for $50,000
After depreciation, it’s valued at $20,000

👉 ACV may only pay $20,000
👉 Replacement Cost may cover closer to full replacement

👉 We Help You Choose the Right Valuation Strategy
⚠️ COINSURANCE CLAUSE (COMPLIANCE + AUTHORITY)
What Most Agents Don’t Explain (But We Do)
Many Commercial Property policies include a coinsurance clause.

This means:
👉 You must insure your property to a certain percentage of its value (often 80–100%)

If you don’t:
👉 You may be penalized during a claim

Example:
Your building is worth $500,000
You only insure it for $250,000

Now you have a partial loss…
👉 The insurance company may reduce your payout
