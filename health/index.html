Health Insurance Basics

The Affordable Care Act and Marketplace coverage, simplified.

The Affordable Care Act changed how many people shop for coverage by expanding access, standardizing protections, and making individual and family plans easier to compare in one place.

ACA Foundations

The ACA expanded access to coverage, standardized key protections, and made it harder for insurers to deny or reprice applicants because of pre-existing conditions.

Essential Benefits

Preventive care, emergency services, prescription drugs, maternity and newborn care, and mental health services were all called out as core examples of required benefits.

Plan Levels

Four tiers, four different cost tradeoffs.

Each metal tier balances monthly premiums against what you may pay when you actually use care. The right fit depends on how often you expect to need doctors, specialists, prescriptions, or ongoing treatment.

Bronze

Lower monthly premiums, but more out of pocket when you actually use care. Often best for shoppers who want the lightest monthly payment.

Silver

A middle ground on premium and usage cost. Silver plans can also unlock extra cost-sharing help for eligible households.

Gold

Higher premiums up front, with lower costs when care is used. A stronger fit for people expecting more regular doctor visits or prescriptions.

Platinum

The highest premium tier, but the lowest out-of-pocket burden at the point of care. Usually built for very frequent medical use.

Cost Terms

The words shoppers need before they compare quotes.

Before comparing plans, it helps to understand the three cost terms that most directly shape how a policy feels in everyday use.

Deductible

The amount you generally pay before the plan starts sharing more of the cost for covered care.

Copays and Coinsurance

Your share when you receive care. Depending on the plan, that may be a flat fee or a percentage of the service cost.

Out-of-Pocket Maximum

The cap on what you pay for covered services in a plan year before the plan takes on more of the cost.

Premium Tax Credit

How premium support can make coverage more affordable.

Premium Tax Credits can lower monthly costs for eligible households shopping through the Marketplace. Qualification typically depends on income, household size, and access to other affordable coverage.

What the subsidy can do

It can lower monthly premiums in advance and then gets reconciled later when taxes are filed. Keeping household and income details current helps the estimate stay accurate.

Who it is aimed at

It is most relevant for low- and moderate-income households buying their own individual or family coverage, especially when affordable employer coverage is not available.
