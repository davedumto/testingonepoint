Coverage Types

Three different paths for income protection.

Disability protection is not one-size-fits-all. Different policies are built for different recovery windows, work situations, and financial responsibilities.

Short-Term Disability

Built for temporary interruptions from illness, injury, surgery, or maternity leave, with support that helps protect cash flow when life pauses unexpectedly.

Long-Term Disability

Designed for longer recoveries from conditions like stroke, cancer, chronic illness, injuries, or mental health events, with benefits that can continue for months or years.

Business Overhead Expense

Created for owners and key professionals who need the business to keep paying rent, payroll, utilities, and fixed bills while they are unable to work.

Long-Term Disability

For recoveries that extend beyond a brief interruption.

Long-term disability is designed for situations where recovery lasts months or longer, helping replace a portion of income after a waiting period and creating breathing room during extended disruption.

Why it matters

Helps pay the mortgage, bills, and household obligations when work is off the table for an extended stretch.

Trigger events

Illness and injury are both major factors, including chronic conditions, cancer, stroke, and mental health-related disability.

Timing

Many long-term disability plans begin benefits after a waiting period of roughly 90 to 180 days.

Features

Own-occupation protection, rider flexibility, and reliable long-duration income support can make the policy more adaptable.

Short-Term Disability

For the income gap between healthy routine and full recovery.

Short-term disability supports the gap between being unable to work and being fully back on your feet, especially when daily expenses do not slow down just because recovery begins.

Who it helps

Employees, employers, expecting parents, and small to mid-sized businesses can all benefit from coverage that protects income during temporary interruptions.

Possible riders

Options can include own occupation, residual disability, catastrophic disability, critical illness, guaranteed insurability, return of premium, benefit increases, non-cancelable coverage, and waiver of premium.

Business Overhead Expense

Protection for the business, not just the owner.

Business overhead coverage is built around a simple goal: keep fixed operating expenses covered while the owner is temporarily unable to work.

Keep your business running, even when you can’t

Overhead expense coverage handles rent, payroll, and fixed bills while you recover.

What it pays for

Rent, utilities, internet, insurance premiums, lease payments, professional fees, and wages for non-owner employees can all be part of the protected expense mix.

What it does not do

It is designed to cover fixed business expenses and preserve operations, not replace business profits.

Why owners use it

It buys time to recover, bring in temporary help, or transition responsibility without the business immediately collapsing under fixed expenses.

Typical structure

Monthly reimbursement after a waiting period, usually for a shorter benefit duration such as 12 to 24 months.
